What Happened?
In 2017, Equifax—one of the three major U.S. credit reporting agencies—suffered a massive data breach after hackers exploited a known vulnerability in its web application software.
The breach occurred between May and July 2017, but was not disclosed until September.

What Was Compromised?
The personal data of 147.9 million Americans, along with tens of millions of British and Canadian citizens, was exposed. The stolen information included:
Full names
Social Security numbers
Birth dates
Addresses
Driver’s license numbers
Around 209,000 credit card numbers

Consequences
Legal & Financial: Equifax reached a settlement of up to $700 million with the U.S. government, including $425 million for consumer compensation.
Leadership Changes: Several executives resigned, including the CEO.
Public Backlash: Equifax was heavily criticized for poor security, a slow response, and unclear communication.
Lasting Impact: Public trust in data handlers declined sharply, and the breach became a driving force in discussions about stricter data privacy laws.

Security and Industry Impact
Equifax invested $1.6 billion to improve its cybersecurity.
The breach emphasized the urgent need for better data protection and spurred proposed privacy regulations in the U.S.
