Technical Logistics of the Attack

Vulnerability Exploited: CVE-2017-5638
The breach originated from CVE-2017-5638, a critical vulnerability in the Apache Struts 2 framework, specifically within its Jakarta Multipart parser used to handle file uploads. This flaw allowed remote code execution (RCE) through crafted HTTP requests containing malicious Content-Type headers.
  • Severity: CVSS score of 10.0 (critical)
  • Disclosure Date: March 6, 2017
  • Patch Released: Same day by the Apache Software Foundation

Equifax’s Failure to Patch

Despite receiving alerts from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and internal security directives, Equifax failed to identify and patch all vulnerable systems. A critical web application tied to Equifax's consumer dispute portal remained unpatched and publicly accessible.
  • March 7, 2017: DHS issues public warning
  • March 8, 2017: Equifax security team is notified
  • March 10, 2017: Attackers begin exploiting the vulnerability
This oversight exposed Equifax to active exploitation for over two months.

Timeline of Exploitation

May 13 – July 30, 2017: Attackers maintained persistent access to Equifax’s network

Data Exfiltrated: Names, Social Security numbers, birthdates, addresses, driver's license numbers, and credit card information
Attackers used web shells and performed lateral movement within the network to escalate access
Exfiltration was performed in small, encrypted batches to avoid detection

Operational Tactics

  • Use of 40+ IP addresses across various countries
  • Deployment of encrypted communication channels
  • Use of anti-forensic techniques, such as log deletion
  • Highly targeted, disciplined data exfiltration strategy indicative of nation-state actors

Conclusion

The Equifax breach was caused by a failure to patch a known, critical vulnerability—CVE-2017-5638—despite multiple warnings and available fixes. The attack was executed by sophisticated state-sponsored actors from the Chinese military, whose objective was strategic data acquisition rather than immediate monetary gain.
This incident underscores the catastrophic risk of poor patch management, especially in critical infrastructure, and serves as a landmark case linking cybersecurity negligence to national security threats.
